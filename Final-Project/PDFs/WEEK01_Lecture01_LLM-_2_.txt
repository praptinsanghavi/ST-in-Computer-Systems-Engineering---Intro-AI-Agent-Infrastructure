LLM
1


´éĘ   Large language models (LLMs), such as those offered in OpenAIÔÇÖs
    ChatGPT, are deep neural network models that have been developed over
    the past few years.
´éĘ   They ushered in a new era for natural language processing (NLP).
´éĘ   Before the advent of LLMs, traditional methods excelled at categorization
    tasks such as email spam classification and straightforward pattern
    recognition that could be captured with handcrafted rules or simpler models.
´éĘ   However, they typically underperformed in language tasks that demanded
    complex understanding and generation abilities, such as parsing detailed
    instructions, conducting contextual analysis, and creating coherent and
    contextually appropriate original text.
´éĘ   For example, previous generations of language models could not write an
    email from a list of keywordsÔÇöa task that is trivial for contemporary LLMs.
LLMs generate and interpret human language
2


´éĘ   LLMs have remarkable capabilities to understand, generate, and interpret
    human language.

´éĘ   However, itÔÇÖs important to clarify that when we say language models
    ÔÇťunderstand,ÔÇŁ we mean that they can process and generate text in ways
    that appear coherent and contextually relevant, not that they possess
    human-like consciousness or comprehension.
LLMs are trained on vast quantities of text data.
3


´éĘ   Enabled by advancements in deep learning, which is a subset of machine
    learning and artificial intelligence (AI) focused on neural networks, LLMs are
    trained on vast quantities of text data.

´éĘ   This large-scale training allows LLMs to capture deeper contextual
    information and subtleties of human language compared to previous
    approaches.

´éĘ   As a result, LLMs have significantly improved performance in a wide range
    of NLP tasks, including text translation, sentiment analysis, question
    answering, and many more.
NLP models were typically designed for specific tasks
4


´éĘ   Another important distinction between contemporary LLMs and earlier NLP
    models is that earlier NLP models were typically designed for specific tasks,
    such as text categorization, language translation, etc.
´éĘ   While those earlier NLP models excelled in their narrow applications, LLMs
    demonstrate a broader proficiency across a wide range of NLP tasks.
´éĘ   The success behind LLMs can be attributed to the transformer architecture that
    underpins many LLMs and the vast amounts of data on which LLMs are
    trained, allowing them to capture a wide variety of linguistic nuances, contexts,
    and patterns that would be challenging to encode manually.
´éĘ   This shift toward implementing models based on the transformer architecture
    and using large training datasets to train LLMs has fundamentally transformed
    NLP, providing more capable tools for understanding and interacting with
    human language.
What is an LLM?
5


´éĘ   An LLM is a neural network designed to understand, generate, and respond
    to human-like text.

´éĘ   These models are deep neural networks trained on massive amounts of text
    data, sometimes encompassing large portions of the entire publicly
    available text on the internet.
The ÔÇťlargeÔÇŁ in ÔÇťLarge Language ModelÔÇŁ
6


´éĘ   The ÔÇťlargeÔÇŁ in ÔÇťlarge language modelÔÇŁ refers to both the modelÔÇÖs size in
    terms of parameters and the immense dataset on which itÔÇÖs trained.

´éĘ   Models like this often have tens or even hundreds of billions of parameters,
    which are the adjustable weights in the network that are optimized during
    training to predict the next word in a sequence.

´éĘ   Next-word prediction is sensible because it harnesses the inherent
    sequential nature of language to train models on understanding context,
    structure, and relationships within text.

´éĘ   Yet, it is a very simple task, and so it is surprising to many researchers that
    it can produce such capable models.
Transformer
7


´éĘ   LLMs utilize an architecture called the transformer, which allows them to
    pay selective attention to different parts of the input when making
    predictions, making them especially adept at handling the nuances and
    complexities of human language.

´éĘ   Since LLMs are capable of generating text, LLMs are also often referred to
    as a form of generative artificial intelligence, often abbreviated as
    generative AI or GenAI.

´éĘ   As illustrated in figure next slide, AI encompasses the broader field of

11


´éĘ    Returning to the spam classification example, in traditional ML, human experts

     trigger words (i.e., ÔÇťprize,ÔÇŁ ÔÇťwin,ÔÇŁ ÔÇťfreeÔÇŁ), the number of exclamation marks, use

Submodules
26


´éĘ    The transformer architecture consists of two submodules:
     ´éĄ an encoder
        ´ü« It processes the input text and encodes it into a series of numerical
           representations/vectors that capture the contextual information of the input.
     ´éĄ a decoder
        ´ü« It takes these encoded vectors and generates the output text.

´éĘ    In a translation task, for example, the encoder would encode the text from the
     source language into vectors, and the decoder would decode these vectors to
     generate text in the target language.

´éĘ    Both the encoder and decoder consist of many layers connected by a so-called
     self-attention mechanism.

´éĘ    How the inputs are preprocessed and encoded will be addressed in a step-by-
     step implementation in the following lectures.
Self-attention mechanism
27


´éĘ    A key component of transformers and LLMs is the self-attention mechanism
     (not shown), which allows the model to weigh the importance of different
     words or tokens in a sequence relative to each other.

´éĘ    This mechanism enables the model to capture long-range dependencies
     and contextual relationships within the input data, enhancing its ability to
     generate coherent and contextually relevant output.

´éĘ    However, due to its complexity, we will defer further explanation to the later
     lectures, where we will discuss and implement it step by step.

´éĘ    Compared to the original transformer architecture, the general GPT

´éĘ    Essentially, itÔÇÖs just the decoder part without the encoder (figure 1.8).

     future predictions.

     that precedes it, which improves the coherence of the resulting text.
